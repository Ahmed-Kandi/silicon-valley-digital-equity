# H1 SVDE Website Brief

## H2 PROJECT OVERVIEW
Formal name: Silicon Valley Digital Equity
Brand name: Silicon Valley Digital Equity
Abbreviation: SVDE
URL/code references: svdigitalequity.org
Type: High school student-led nonprofit
Fiscal Sponsor: Hack Club Bank (501c3, pending approval)
Location: San Jose / Cupertino, CA (Silicon Valley)


## H2 MISSION
Bridge the digital divide in Silicon Valley by refurbishing donated computers
and distributing them free to low-income families. Despite being one of the
wealthiest tech regions in the world, many families in our community still
lack access to basic computing equipment.


## H2 HOW IT WORKS
1. We collect retired computers from local businesses (accounting firms, law
  offices, real estate agencies) who donate hardware they would otherwise
  discard or pay to e-waste.

2. We wipe, repair, and install free open-source software on each machine.
  Parts cost roughly $20-40 per build; the hardware itself is donated.

3. Families referred through our partnership with Second Harvest of Silicon
  Valley receive a fully functional computer at no cost.


## H2 CURRENT STATUS
- Pre-launch; first builds planned for Summer 2026
- First cohort goal: 5-10 computers
- Fiscal sponsor: Hack Club Bank (pending approval)
- Community partner: Second Harvest of Silicon Valley
- Team: High school student from San Jose / Cupertino area


## H2 LONG-TERM VISION
Build a replicable chapter model with a documented playbook that other high
school students across the Bay Area can use to run their own SVDE chapter,
scaling the program regionally without depending on one team.


## H2 KEY MESSAGES
- The Silicon Valley paradox: world's richest tech hub, families without computers
- Direct traceable impact: 1 business donation = 1 family gets a computer
- Student-led but serious: real partners, real operation, not a school project
- All monetary donations are tax-deductible via Hack Club's 501(c)(3)
